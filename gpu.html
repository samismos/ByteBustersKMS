Graphics Processing Unit (GPU)

Why is it so important?

In terms of both personal and business computing, the GPU has emerged as one of the most significant categories of computing technology. The GPU, which was created for parallel processing, is employed in a variety of tasks, including generating images and videos. GPUs are becoming increasingly widely used in creative production and artificial intelligence, despite their best-known employment in gaming (AI). GPUs were initially created to speed up 3D visual rendering. They improved their capacities as they become more adaptable and programmable. This made it possible for graphic designers to produce more eye-catching visual effects and realistic scenarios using cutting-edge lighting and shadowing methods. In order to significantly speed up extra workloads in high performance computing (HPC), deep learning, and other areas, other developers also started to take advantage of GPU power.

BB Chapter 5: A look at Graphics Processing Units

Graphics Processing Unit (GPU)

A GPU (or Graphics Processing Unit) is a specialized type of processor designed to handle the complex calculations required for rendering images and video.

What is it made of?

A GPU is made out of thousands of small cores, compared to a CPU which typically has a few powerful cores.

Are there many types of GPUs?

There are different types of GPUs, such as consumer-grade GPUs and professional-grade GPUs, each with their own strengths and use cases.

So it's the same as a CPU?

GPUs are much more powerful than a typical CPU (Central Processing Unit) for certain types of workloads, such as parallel computing and machine learning.

Where are they used?

GPUs are commonly used in gaming PCs, workstations, and data centers for tasks such as scientific simulations, rendering, and machine learning.

Which companies make GPUs?

The most popular GPU manufacturers are Nvidia, AMD and as of 2022, Intel, but there are other companies that produce GPUs as well.

How much power does it consume?

The power consumption of a GPU can be quite high, which is one of the reasons why they are typically used in desktops and servers, rather than laptops or mobile devices.

It's a tight market

Ever since the 2020 pandemic, GPU prices have skyrocketed. It's a good idea to examine the market closely before making any decisions, since it's almost impossible to find a cheap GPU anymore.
