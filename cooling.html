Cooling

Why is it so important?

Many PC builders already know this, but it might be helpful to explore why cooling is an essential component of a build and to apply these principles to new builds. The fundamentals are straightforward: intense workloads, such as gaming, cause technology to produce heat. Performance problems may be caused by overheated components. An optimum configuration allows you to get the best performance out of your system and keeps all of its components cool enough. Although heat is a natural result of operating computer hardware, too much of it can slow down the entire system.

Cooling

Cooling is an essential aspect of PC building and maintenance as it prevents overheating and damage to the components.

How does it work?

The most common types of cooling for PCs are air cooling and liquid cooling. Let's check them out!

Air cooling

Air cooling uses fans to circulate air through the PC case and over the hot components, such as the CPU and GPU. This can include using a single fan or multiple fans, as well as using larger or more powerful fans.

Water cooling

Liquid cooling uses a liquid, typically water or a water-based solution, to transfer heat away from the hot components. This can include using a closed loop system or a custom loop system.

CPU cooler

The CPU cooler is the most important component in the cooling system, it can be either a air cooler or liquid cooler.

What is thermal paste?

The thermal paste, also known as thermal interface material, is a substance that fills the microscopic gaps between the CPU and the cooler, it's important to use high quality thermal paste for better thermal conductivity.

The silent killer

Dust can accumulate in the PC case and on the cooling components, which can reduce their effectiveness. It's important to periodically clean the inside of the PC case and the cooling components.

Key to prevention

Monitoring the temperature of the components is important, this can be done by using software such as SpeedFan or HWMonitor which can help identify any potential cooling issues.
