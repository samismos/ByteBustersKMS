Storage

Why is it so important?

Data storage refers to any techniques and tools used to record and keep digital data on magnetic, optical, or silicon-based storage media. Offices, data centers, edge settings, remote locations, and private residences all need storage. Mobile gadgets like smartphones and tablets need storage as a key component. Storage is a resource that both consumers and organizations use to protect information, from private images to data that is essential to their operations. Devices that connect to a computer, either directly or through a network, and facilitate data transmission through input/output (I/O) operations are usually referred to as storage. Hard disk drives (HDDs), flash-based solid-state drives (SSDs), optical disc drives, tape systems, and other media types can all be used as storage devices.

BB Chapter 4: Storage Wars

Computer storage

We refer to computer storage as any device which is capable of permanently storing data, such as floppy disks, CDs, HDDs and SSDs and more.

Isn't that the same as memory?

No, since memory (RAM) loses all its data when it stops being provided with electricity, while storage maintains the data even while powered off.

History lesson

Perhaps you've seen these at your grandma's house. These used to be the standard for storing data like files, movies, songs etc. We are now equipped with much more capable technology.

What do we use now?

As of 2023, the most popular options for storage are Hard Drives (HDDs), Solid State Drives (SSDs) and USB flash drives. All of these are different and come with their own advantages and disadvantages. Let's start exploring.

HDD

A hard disk drive, or HDD, is a disk which stores and loads data with the use of a magnetic head. It is quite slow compared to the other options, but it is extremely cost-efficient and thus an excellent storage solution for bulky cold storage such as image, audio and video files.

SSD

The solid state drives, or SSDs, are split into two categories based on their connection protocol: SATA and NVMe. While both are at least 5-7 times faster than an HDD, NVMe SSDs have overtaken the market because of their small size, lack of physical wires and dazzling speed.

What are form factors?

Let's clear that up. HDDs mainly come in 2 form factors: 3,5" and 2,5". This refers to their size in inches. Look at the image on the left.

SSD form factors

SSDs mostly come in the 2,5" and mSATA form factors, but they also appear in the M.2 format. It's important to understand that the form factor DOES NOT define the communication protocol. There are plenty of M.2 SSDs that use SATA.

Which one should I buy?

If you're planning to use your computer to store a lot of static data like photos, videos, movies and music an HDD will cost you less. However, if you're planning to run demanding applications such as gaming and video editing, an SSD will significantly increase your performance and reduce the loading times.
