Power Supply Unit (PSU)

Why is it so important?

A computer's internal components receive low-voltage, regulated DC power from a power supply unit (PSU), which converts mains AC. Switched-mode power supply are utilized by all contemporary personal computers. While some power supplies automatically adjust to the mains voltage, others offer a manual switch for choosing the input voltage. The ATX specification, which specifies form factor and voltage tolerances, is followed by the majority of contemporary desktop personal computer power supplies. The 5-volt standby (5VSB) power that an ATX power supply always supplies when it is connected to the mains supply powers the computer's standby features and some peripherals. A signal from the motherboard turns on and off ATX power supplies.

Power Supply Unit (PSU)

A power supply, also known as a PSU (power supply unit), is a component that supplies power to the various components of a PC.

What does it do?

The PSU converts the alternating current (AC) from the wall outlet into the direct current (DC) that the PC components require.

Watts?

The wattage rating of a PSU indicates the maximum amount of power it can supply. It's important to choose a PSU with a wattage rating that meets or exceeds the power requirements of your PC components.

How does it fit with everything else?

The most common PSU connectors are ATX, AT and SFX, which are used to connect to the motherboard, drives, and other components. It's important to ensure that the PSU you choose has the appropriate connectors for your system.

What is "80+ certified"?

The efficiency rating of a PSU, usually represented as 80 Plus, indicates how much of the power drawn from the wall outlet is converted into usable power for the PC. A higher efficiency rating can result in lower energy costs and less heat generated by the PSU.

Suspiciously cheap?

It's important to choose a reputable brand when buying a PSU, as poor quality power supplies can be unreliable and potentially damage your PC components.

What if there's a power outage?

Almost all power supplies from reputable brands come with built-in over-voltage and over-current protection, which can help prevent damage to your PC in the event of a power surge or other electrical problem.
